How Clara Rates Facilities

What the map colors mean, where the data comes from, and how we keep it honest.

The Short Version

Every pin on Clara's map is a real, licensed care facility in Washington State. The color of the pin tells you how their most recent state inspection went. That's it. No opinion, no guesswork. Just what the state found.

What the Colors Mean

🟢 Green — No Findings

The state inspected this facility and found nothing wrong. Clean bill of health on their latest inspection. This is the best result a facility can get.

🟡 Yellow — Findings Corrected

Inspectors found some issues, but the facility fixed them. Think of it like getting a note from the teacher but doing your homework to make it right. The problems were real, but they've been resolved.

🔴 Red — Findings Uncorrected

Inspectors found problems and the facility has not yet fixed them as of their last inspection. This doesn't automatically mean the facility is bad, but it does mean there are open issues the state is tracking. Worth digging into the details before making a decision.

⚪ Gray — No Recent Data

We don't have a recent inspection on file for this facility. Either their last inspection was over 12 months ago, or the state hasn't published the results yet. Gray doesn't mean good or bad. It just means we can't tell you right now.

🔵 Blue — Your Location

This one's just you! When you search for facilities near an address, the blue pin marks the center of your search. It's your home base on the map.

The Verified Badge

Some facilities have a blue Verified badge next to their name. This means the facility has actively signed up with Clara and confirmed their information. Verified facilities can update their bed availability, share their capabilities, and keep their listing current.

The verified badge says "this facility is engaged and responsive." It does not change their color rating. A verified facility with uncorrected findings will still show up red. The badge is about participation, not a quality endorsement.

On the map, verified facilities get a subtle blue glow around their pin so you can spot them easily.

Where the Data Comes From

Everything starts with the Washington State Department of Social and Health Services (DSHS). They're the agency that licenses and inspects every adult family home, assisted living facility, and enhanced services facility in the state.

When DSHS inspects a facility, they write up a report. These reports are public record. They list exactly what inspectors looked at, what they found (if anything), and whether the facility corrected the issues. Clara reads these reports so you don't have to.

How We Turn Reports Into Colors

1

Download the inspection report

Clara pulls the actual PDF documents from the state's public database. These are the same reports you could find yourself on the DSHS website, but scattered across thousands of pages.

2

Read and understand the text

Some reports are typed, some are scanned paper documents. Clara handles both. For scanned pages, we use optical character recognition (OCR) to read the text from images. For mixed documents (part typed, part scanned), we combine both methods to make sure nothing gets missed.

3

Summarize with AI

An AI model reads the full inspection text and extracts the key facts: what deficiencies were found, how serious they were, what rules were cited, and most importantly, whether the facility corrected the problems. The AI produces a structured summary that Clara can work with.

4

Use the most recent result

A facility might have multiple inspections over the years. Clara uses the most recent report to determine the color. Why? Because people change. Facilities improve (or don't). What matters most is where things stand right now, not what happened three years ago.

5

Assign the color

Based on the latest report's overall result, Clara assigns the pin color. "No Findings" = green. "Findings Corrected" = yellow. "Findings Uncorrected" = red. If the data is over a year old, the pin turns gray.

A note about multi-document reports: Sometimes DSHS bundles multiple documents into one PDF. For example, an initial inspection report might be followed by a compliance letter saying the facility fixed everything. When that happens, Clara treats the compliance letter as the final word, because that's exactly what it is: the state's official follow-up confirming the issues were resolved.

The Sentiment Rating

When you click on a facility, you'll see a sentiment rating: Excellent, Good, Fair, Poor, or Concerning. This looks at the bigger picture across all available inspections, not just the latest one.

  • Excellent — Zero deficiencies found across all inspections. Spotless record.
  • Good — Only minor issues that were quickly corrected. Nothing to worry about.
  • Fair — Some moderate issues found. Not great, not terrible.
  • Poor — Serious issues or uncorrected problems. Worth a closer look.
  • Concerning — Critical or repeated uncorrected problems. Proceed with caution.

You'll also see a trend (Improving, Stable, or Declining) that compares recent inspections to older ones. A facility rated "Fair" but trending "Improving" is heading in the right direction.

What Clara Doesn't Do

We believe in being upfront about what this is and isn't:

  • Clara does not rank facilities. Two green-pinned facilities are not automatically equal. One might be a perfect fit for your family and the other might not. The colors tell you about inspections, not about whether your mom will like the food.
  • Clara does not replace visiting in person. No amount of data can tell you what it feels like to walk through the front door. Use Clara to narrow down your list, then go see the places yourself.
  • Clara does not guarantee accuracy. We do our best to read the state's reports correctly, but these are complex legal documents and our AI, while good, isn't perfect. When in doubt, read the original inspection report (we link to it from each facility's detail page).
  • Clara is not endorsed by DSHS. We're an independent tool that uses publicly available government data. The state doesn't review or approve our ratings.

How Often Is the Data Updated?

Clara checks for new inspection reports every night. When the state publishes a new report, Clara picks it up, reads it, and updates the facility's color. Most facilities are inspected once a year, so don't expect the colors to change frequently. That's normal.
